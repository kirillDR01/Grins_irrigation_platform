Grins CRM · Schedule UX v2 — "Pick Jobs to Schedule"

Group and filter in one control

v1 gave you a Group-by menu with City / Tag / Priority. The question this v2 answers: once you group by City, how do you say “but only these 3 cities”? Or “group by tag, but only show VIP and HOA”? Answer: the Group button becomes a cascading picker — pick the dimension, then narrow within it, without leaving the toolbar.

★ Combo 1 — full table + cascading group + unified tray

Every enhanced column — notes, tags, job type, requested, priority, duration, equipment — plus the cascading Group button on the toolbar, plus a persistent scheduling tray with calendar popover, staff picker, default duration, and per-job time adjustments.

What changed

① Group by, 6 modes Week, city, customer, type, tag, priority. City is the scheduler's default mental model — cluster visits = fewer miles.
② Tag pills, in a column VIP, Commercial, HOA, Prepaid, Needs Ladder, Dog on Site, Gated, Recurring. Colored so the scheduler spots them while scanning.
③ Auto-filled city City no longer typed — it's a filterable dropdown populated from customer data. The old free-text "City" field becomes a narrowing filter.
④ Notes inline, not hidden 📝 icon on customers with notes; row click expands a highlighted note row. Gate codes, dog warnings, access quirks surface exactly when routing.

② Week board — see urgency at a glance, drag jobs to a week

Same jobs, reshaped into requested-week columns. An overdue column on the far left surfaces anything past its requested window. Each card shows customer + city + tags. Drop zones make bulk-moving easy; the selection tray still handles staff assignment.

Why a week board

Urgency is spatial Overdue column on the left, future on the right. You see backlog instead of reading a date column.
Drag to reschedule weeks If a customer calls to push out a visit, drag their card — no dialog, no typing.
Tags still visible Same colored pills. Commercial, VIP, Ladder-needed, Dog-on-site all visible at card scale.
Same tray Selection tray behaves identically across all three views. Muscle memory transfers.

③ Map + list — the route is the interface

For route-building days (spring startup, fall winterization — dozens of jobs), the map becomes the primary surface. Click a city cluster, the side list filters to that city. A route distance footer computes mileage for the current selection. Swap to table/week board via the view switch.
