**Updated System/Process Mapping**

**Lead intake:**

We first start off with lead intake which is how the system will bring in all the leads into a centralized place for scheduling. Clients will call/text my number and the system will intake all these leads and guide them to the appropriate area through an AI agent communicating to them. Areas where leads will come from include but are not limited to: 

* **Website traffic and landing pages** (Good buttons and AI text bot agents)  
* **Marketing strategies** (Mass emails, mass text, flyers, google adds, social media adds, are more with a formal link, buttons, or QR codes clients can use)  
* **Calls and texts from google** (Ai agent answering calls/texts and guiding the client)   
* **Anywhere else** (Any previous form of guiding them)

There are two main areas that the system will lead them to:

1. Sign up for seasonal services through tier packages via the website (See price list for seasonal services)  
   1. Clients will have options to choose tier packages   
   2. Clients will select and pay for the package   
   3. System collects client information (demographics, package, available timeframes, and other notes)   
   4. Information is sent to:  
      1. Scheduling dashboard for scheduling   
      2. Accounting for tracking of payment    
   5. Admin will set schedule and have AI reach out to confirm appointment   
2. Work request form for work request outside of the services packages. This will have two separate tag to which include:  
   1. (SCHEDULE) Clients will talk with an AI agent to request services and the AI agent figures out what service or job they want with the price associated with it, it lets them know the price and if they confirm they fill out the work request form. These work request are then sent to scheduling dashboard to start scheduling   
   2. (FOLLOW UP) If AI agent cant figure it out, they have option to speak with technician or fill out work request form and can select the work request and “estimate” so we know a human needs to follow up with them and confirm what they need

Key Considerations: 

- Work requests should be very simple and easy to fill out, we must make sure there is as little friction to filling out his form as possible. Make sure there is a text or email confirmation that the work request has been filled out. 

- All communications to customers throughout this process will be handled by AI agents to eliminate the need for a person to speak with them. Clients will have the option to speak to a real person if the AI agent cannot resolve the issue in their call/text.   
     
- The system should intake or track this key information at the minimum from clients whether its for seasonal service sign up or work request:  
* First name   
* Last name   
* City   
* Address   
* Number   
* Email Address   
* Payment information (if request selected requires prepay)  
* Service selected/tier package selected   
* Agreement to our terms and conditions   
* Approval of offers via email/text   
* Approval of us sending them texts   
* Customer general availability or requested service times 

Once the system takes the request from the client, all service package sign ups and work requests tagged (Schedule) will be sent over to the scheduling dashboard to start scheduling.  All other requests tagged (FOLLOW UP) will be put into a centralized place within the admin dashboard that would only be visible by the admin. Within this section, the admin can review all the requests that need a follow up and contact them in order to figure out what is needed. After the admin follows up with the client, the admin can move the request to the schedule dashboard as either a confirmed job or estimate that needs to be scheduled. If a client does not want to work with us or schedule an estimate, the admin can remove the work request from the follow up section but the system will keep track of this request in the database for future reference if needed. 

KEY CONSIDERATION  
The system will automatically update the other key dashboards with key information where needed and automatically populate/update a central database of information. The central database should be our single source of truth that will retrieve and supply all of our other key dashboards and be used to accurately pull any information we need for future reference. This database will include but not limit itself to the following information:

* Key client information submitted by clients   
* Key client information submitted by us   
* Client service/job history   
* Client payment history   
* Clients special notes, photos, videos, data   
* Clients opt in/out for texting   
* Customer tags 

Once we have the initial requests from clients in and all initial information is gathered, we can then proceed with the next dashboards that will be used to complete the other processes of the complete system.  

**Scheduling** 

The scheduling dashboard is where all approved jobs and requests will be scheduled and assigned to staff/crews. The main goal with this dashboard is to review the jobs that have been approved and need to be completed and understand how much work we have. If we understand how far we are booked out, we can then let customers know roughly what our lead time is and make key decisions on if we need to hire people to handle the work or not. It will also give us an understanding of if we need to ramp up advertising/marketing to increase job flow. All actual scheduling, customer communication, and staffing should be automated and take little to no time/effort other than basic review. **Please ATTACHED DOCS IN ASANA on how the scheduling dashboard should look like.** 

The schedule will be sent to the staff/crews assigned to the job before and after their approval as described in the previous schedule. On the staff/crew end, they will only see their specific schedule and not other staff/crew’s schedules. They will have a schedule with the individual appointment slots and each appointment slot will include key customer information as follows:

* Customer name   
* Customer contact information   
* Job type   
* Location   
* Materials/equipment needed   
* Amount to collect or charge   
* Time given to complete   
* Key client data/history   
* Key client directions (if applicable)   
* Other applicable clients details 

The staff/crew should have a clear understanding of their route that day along with all the key data they need to complete the jobs on time and efficiently. In order to keep track of who is doing what, the admin side should have details about the staff as well to get an understanding of what is going on and make adjustments if needed. This includes but is not limited to: 

* Staff Location (GPS Tracking)  
* Current Job in progress   
* Time left to complete the job  
* The same notes and information as the staff 

Now that the key information is available and presented to both staff and admin, the system should then automate the communication to client about timing which includes but is not limited to:  

* Automatic notification to client the day of job about estimated arrival time   
* Automatic notification to client when staff is on his way and estimated time bounced off of travel time   
* Automatic notification of delays and confirmation of client approval if delays are okay   
* Notification of arrival 

As the staff arrives, the staff will then be responsible to follow a process to complete the job in an effective and timely manner. The staff will have the following process to follow:

* Knock on client’s door or call client if client is not present at the door   
* Review job request with client   
* Adjust prices/service (if applicable)  
* Upsell additional work (if applicable)  
* Start and complete job   
* Present completed job to client (If applicable)  
* Provide additional estimate (If applicable)  
* Collect payment or invoice customer via the portal   
* Request google review if good candidate   
* Update jobs notes (Completion status, amount paid/invoiced, if additional work or estimate is needed, materials used for accounting, other key client or property information)  
* Mark appointment is complete

Each individual appointment slot will have its own client associated with it where the staff will have a place to be able to quickly provide estimates, invoices, request google review, update notes, and whatever else is needed. The staff will additionally have resources like a standard price list and services list that they can use to quickly pull information to build estimates and invoices on the spot. Once the appointment is marked as completed by staff, this system should automatically notify the client of completion and provide the client with key information about the job (Job type, completion status, receipt or invoice, any additional notes). From there, that would be a cue for the system to notify the next client of when they could expect the staff to arrive. There should also be an option for staff to be able to do things like stop by a gas station to fill up if need be. Maybe the client gets a 30-45 minute time window instead of an exact time.     

Additional key elements to consider: 

* Payment will be either collected on the spot via credit card scanner, cash/check or route the client to pay via the portal. The staff should be able to draw up an invoice for the customer quickly and the customer should be able to receive the invoice right away once staff sends via a push notification.   
* If a client requests additional work, a formal estimate should be able to be drawn up quickly and sent to the client for approval. Once approved, the staff can continue with the job. I don't believe writing up an additional contract for them would be very efficient, I believe instead our terms conditions should state something around the lines of “any estimate that is approved is considered as a formal contract agreement between both parties”.   
* If a staff member is spending too much time on an appointment, they will be notified of how much time they have left and if they should move on to the next appointment to avoid staying late on the job and being late to the next one. 


  
The staff will only be updating each individual appointment slot that has a client attached to it. All information updated within will be automatically updated to the main database which will house all this data if needed for future reference along with updating other dashboard if needed. For example, if the staff sends an estimate and the client needs time to approve, the estimate will automatically be updated within the sales dashboard after 2 days of no approval. Or if special instructions require payment to be completed at a different time/way, it will automatically update the accounting dashboard. If jobs need to be scheduled again, it will automatically be sent to the scheduling dashboard. 

**Sales** 

Sales information will also be housed in the admin dashboard. All the information needed to be able to take in leads and sell them a job before it gets scheduled. This will likely mainly house bigger projects like installs and landscaping projects, but it may also include smaller jobs that just need a face to the estimate and approval. It should include but is not limited to:

* Estimates that still need to be written up and sent   
* Estimates that are pending approval   
* Estimates that need follow up   
* Revenue to be gained from approval of estimates 

A sales staff will have access to this dashboard and has the ability to communicate and schedule these estimates along with the admin. The scheduling process will be similar to the approved job scheduling process and sales staff arrival notifications to clients will follow the same process as well. Once a sales staff arrives on the estimate, the staff will consult with the client and be able to use the sales dashboard to build the client an estimate on the spot. This feature will include but is not limited to:

* Estimate templates to be used to build ready to go estimates on the spot  
* Estimate price adjustment options based on materials used/system design giving the client the ability to pick an option that will best fit their budget. This should have key details about pluses and minus for the client that they can review   
* Past videos, photos, and customer testimonials used to show clients what they are being sold and what they will achieve with purchasing the product.   
* Basic level diagram or vision that could be built on the spot quickly of the current property and system design that the customer can review. This does not require to be anything fancy, it can simply be a birds eye view of their property with the rough view of the system. Another good idea would be to potentially implement an AI feature to be able to take photos of a specific area like a mulch bed and request the AI to change the colors of the mulch for example so customers can see what different colors look like.    
* Estimate promotions if applicable 

As the 1 on 1 meeting ends, all the above should be able to be provided to the customer at the end along with a link to approve and sign a contract for the estimate.. The goal would be to get all this done during the sales meeting process without needing to provide anything additional post the 1 on 1 meeting. The customer will be able to review all the different documents, photos, videos, etc. provided during the estimate and help them make a decision of what they want. This should be high quality and very professional but very simple as this is the first thing the client will see and first impressions matter. If the client sees a very well put together estimate and estimate process and has a clear vision sold to them, they are more willing to go with us. As the sales staff marks the appointment complete, the next client in their schedule should be notified of the rough arrival time of the sales staff similar to before. 

Post meeting, the sales staff will be able to use the dashboard to click into each individual estimate and review key information about a specific estimate. Key information that the sales staff will be able to review and edit includes but is not limited to: 

* Estimate provided   
* Price of estimate   
* Applicable diagrams/photos/videos   
* Contract(s) provided   
* Customer information   
* Last contact date   
* Notes based on previous discussion   
* 

The sales staff will be able to use this information to follow up with individual customers to try to sell them on the project and get the customer to turn over. The sales staff should have promotional options available to use to try to convince the customer to agree to the estimate. The sales staff will also have an automated notification option which they can select for a given estimate to automatically send push notifications to selected clients reminding them of the estimate and trying to get them to approve. The automated function will be sent out every set amount of days until either the customer approves or disapproves the estimate.  

As with all the other dashboards, all items within here will be automatically updated to the central database to be referenced for the future if applicable. 

**Accounting** 

The accounting section will only be able to be accessed and viewed by the admin and house all the numbers to be able to gain key insights on profit margins, customer spending data, and other key insights. It will also house important information on invoicing data. This dashboard will mainly be used by an admin to review key accounting data.  

On the general dashboard, the admin will be able to view key metrics and get a general understanding of accounting. The general dashboard will include but is not limited to:

* Year to date profit (options to go further out than a year)  
* Year to date revenue (options to go further out than a year)  
* Pending invoices within the allotted payment period (includes total amount pending)  
* Past due invoices (includes total amount pending)  
* Spending metrics per category   
* Average profit margin


  
The dashboard should have an invoicing section to review key invoice data. In terms of the pending invoices, the system will automatically notify customers through a push notification 3 days before the invoice is past due and weekly after the invoice is past due. In our terms and conditions, we should have a section that discusses past due invoice late fees and mechanic lien information if the client decides to not pay. If the client does not pay after 30 days, the system will send a formal notification to the property owner about a lien notification notifying clients that a formal lien will be sent if payment is not completed within the next week. **Note, lien notifications have to be sent within 45 days of job completion or materials provided on the job and can only be sent for services that improve the property**. **Services a lien cannot be sent for include start up, winterization, diagnostics, summer tune up. All other services can have a lean sent. Formal liens have 120 days to be sent post job completion or materials provided which will be sent 45 days after invoice is sent. For all seasonal services, payment will be collected up front as we cannot send liens for those services.**   
**If we can, we would also like to have a credit on file to charge a client if need be.** The goal of having this process in place is to have a set system to follow to avoid wasting time collecting payment. 

In terms of key system processes in place to collect and store financial data, the whole goal of this process is to collect information to draw insights for the future if adjustments need to be made to material cost, prices, payment of staff, and whatever else. The financial data part of the  dashboard should include but is not limited to these following items:

* Material cost for each job  
* Staff cost for each job  
* Total money received from the specific job   
* Customer acquisition cost (through marketing)  
* Fuel and maintenance costs of equipment (maybe through a standard cost per hour or mile?)  
* Other costs that could bring key insights 

The system then uses this key information and does the basic math needed to draw up general financial data/job specific financial data. We can do things like connect our credit cards and banking information to the system and use that financial data to collect spending on materials, equipment, fuel, etc. We can also draw metrics like miles needed to travel or equipment hours used from specific jobs that can be collected by staff or the system that can then have a specific multiplier to it (for example IRS does around $0.66 per mile traveled). 

The accounting dashboard should also have a tax section which will help us collect and store all key tax information to be used to effectively and accurately complete our taxes for the season. This will include but it not limited to:

* Material spending amount   
* Insurance spending amount   
* Equipment usage amount   
* Equipment service costs   
* Office materials spending

The system will have a feature in place to take photos and store all receipts throughout the season to avoid losing and counting all receipts during the tax season. It will take those photos and draw the amount from the receipts and then we could select to put it into a specific category. The system will then take in all of this information and give us an estimated total tax amount due that we can reference throughout the season to be able to make key decisions to reduce tax amount due. We should have an option to propose future spending to see how our taxes will be adjusted if we do that spending. The whole goal of this section is to have a clear view throughout the season of our tax situation and be able to make key tax decisions before it's too late.

## DATABASE / DATA MODEL GAPS IDENTIFIED (2026-03-23)

The following schema and infrastructure gaps must be resolved before the system requirements above can be fully implemented. Organized by requirement section.

### Lead Intake Gaps

**Missing columns on `leads` table:**
- `city` (String(100), nullable) — Required to display city at high level. Currently only `zip_code` exists.
- `address` (String(255), nullable) — Required to collect full address from clients per intake requirements.
- `state` (String(50), nullable, default 'MN') — Companion to city/address.
- Customer availability/preferred times are NOT collected on the lead intake form. The `preferred_service_times` field only exists on the `customers` table (post-conversion).

**SentMessage table constraint — blocks lead SMS:**
- `sent_messages.customer_id` is NOT NULL. Cannot store SMS records for leads who haven't been converted to customers.
- No `lead_id` FK exists on `sent_messages` table.
- The CHECK constraint on `message_type` does not include `lead_confirmation`.
- **Fix:** Make `customer_id` nullable, add `lead_id` (UUID FK→leads.id, nullable), add CHECK requiring at least one of customer_id/lead_id to be non-null, add `lead_confirmation` to message_type CHECK.

**Missing: Voice AI integration (Vapi or similar)**
- No voice call handling infrastructure. Twilio SMS works but there is no IVR or conversational voice AI for inbound calls.
- Required for: "AI agent answering calls/texts and guiding the client"

**Missing: Public-facing website chatbot**
- AI chat components exist for internal dashboard only. No customer-facing chatbot widget for the website.
- Required for: "Good buttons and AI text bot agents" on website

### Scheduling Gaps

**Missing columns on `appointments` table:**
- `en_route_at` (DateTime(timezone=True), nullable) — Timestamp for "On my way" status. Currently only `arrived_at` and `completed_at` exist, making 3-phase time tracking impossible.
- `payment_status` (String(50), nullable) — Values: pending, collected, invoiced, waived. Required to enforce "staff can't complete until payment collected or invoice sent."
- `invoice_id` (UUID FK→invoices.id, nullable) — Link invoice created on-site to the specific appointment. Currently no direct relationship between appointments and invoices.

**AppointmentStatus enum mismatch (LIVE BUG):**
- Backend Python enum has 5 values: scheduled, confirmed, in_progress, completed, cancelled
- Frontend TypeScript type has 7 values: adds `pending` and `no_show`
- `no_show` is actively used in frontend (`markNoShow()` function) but will fail the DB CHECK constraint when sent to backend. **This is a live bug.**
- `en_route` does not exist in either backend or frontend. Was designed in `.kiro/specs/crm-gap-closure/design.md` but never implemented.
- **Fix:** Add `pending`, `en_route`, `no_show` to Python enum + create migration to update DB CHECK constraint to include all 8 values.

**Missing table: `staff_locations`**
- No GPS tracking infrastructure. Required for: "Staff Location (GPS Tracking)", "Current Job in progress", "Time left to complete the job."
- Suggested: `staff_locations` table with staff_id, latitude, longitude, accuracy_meters, recorded_at.
- Frontend would use Browser Geolocation API; backend stores periodic updates.

**Missing table: `notification_schedules`**
- No automated recurring notification infrastructure. All notifications are currently manual one-off actions.
- Required for: day-of appointment notifications, "on my way" ETA to client, delay notifications, arrival notifications, completion notifications with receipt/invoice.
- Suggested: `notification_schedules` table with entity_type, entity_id, notification_type, channel (sms/email), next_send_at, interval_days, max_sends, sends_completed, is_active.

**Missing table: `documents`/`attachments`**
- No file storage for staff field photos, job documentation, before/after images.
- Required for: "Staff add customer notes and photos", completion evidence, property documentation.
- Requires object storage service (AWS S3 or DigitalOcean Spaces, ~$5/mo).

**Missing table: `google_review_requests`**
- No tracking for Google review solicitation.
- Required for: "Request google review if good candidate" in staff workflow.
- Suggested: customer_id, appointment_id, sent_via, sent_at, review_link, status (sent/clicked/reviewed).

**Missing: Invoice/estimate creation from appointment workflow**
- Invoice system exists separately (`/features/invoices/`) but is not accessible from appointment view.
- Estimate system does not exist at all (see Sales Gaps below).
- Required for: staff quick invoice creation with payment links, staff quick estimate creation on the spot.

**Missing: Time-per-appointment warning system**
- Timestamps (`arrived_at`, `completed_at`) are tracked but no alerting or warning logic exists.
- Required for: "If a staff member is spending too much time, they will be notified."

**Missing: Staff break/pause functionality**
- No break/stop concept in appointment workflow.
- Required for: "Staff to be able to do things like stop by a gas station."
- Could be modeled as a special appointment type or a `breaks` table.

### Cross-Cutting Infrastructure Gaps

**Object Storage Service — DOES NOT EXIST**
- Zero file upload capability in the entire system. No S3, GCS, Cloudinary, or any file storage.
- Required for: customer photos, staff field photos, receipt photos, invoice PDFs, estimate PDFs, contract PDFs, property diagrams, portfolio media.
- Recommended: AWS S3 or DigitalOcean Spaces (~$5/mo). Need `documents`/`attachments` table + upload endpoints + signed URL generation.

**PDF Generation — DOES NOT EXIST**
- No PDF generation library or capability. Invoices, estimates, contracts, and receipts are all data-only DB records.
- Required for: professional downloadable invoices, emailable estimates, signable contracts, completion receipts.
- Recommended: WeasyPrint (HTML→PDF using existing Jinja2 templates) or ReportLab.

**Error Tracking / Monitoring — DOES NOT EXIST**
- No Sentry, Rollbar, or any error tracking service integrated.
- Not in requirements but strongly recommended for production stability.
